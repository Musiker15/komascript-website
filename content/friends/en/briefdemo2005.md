For LinuxTag 2005, I created a presentation on the topic “LaTeX as a Synonym
for the Separation of Form and Content.” The presentation, created using
[`beamer`](https://www.ctan.org/pkg/beamer), uses letters with different
layouts as examples to demonstrate just how far the separation of form and
content can go in LaTeX.

Even for people who weren’t at LinuxTag, the source files for this
presentation may be of interest, as they contain various LCO files for
different letter layouts and can also serve as letter examples.

Both the source code and the finished presentation can be downloaded in their
latest versions from the [project's release page on
Codeberg](https://codeberg.org/komascript/briefdemo2005/releases), but note,
that the whole presentation is in German.
