A few minutes ago, I received an email in which an acquaintance and
self-proclaimed KOMA-Script user asked me point-blank if I'd gone crazy. Well,
that's a good question.

The background was that I’d told this person about all the *volunteer* work I
do—ranging from my church involvement to various *“Hey, can you watch our two
youngest kids on Saturday?”—“Sure, why not!”*—to my open-source work, and even
to: *“The template for `https://bitsandbytesoninternetarepatient.invalid` is
sooo off the mark when it comes to how you’re supposed to do things with
KOMA-Script; I absolutely have to set aside a day to show that poor user who
fell for it how it’s really done.”* When was the last time I read a book?
Hard to believe—two weeks ago!

Anyway, this person didn’t just want to ask me outrageous questions—he also
wanted to do me a favor yet again. When I half-heartedly declined and sent him
a link to my (neglected) Amazon [wish list](/en/docs/koma/wishlist), he immediately said
I shouldn’t keep it such a secret, but rather put a big, clear link to
komascript.de—preferably right at the top of the page, in bold giant letters
and red. The latter was a bit too much for me, especially since I’d recently
hidden the link in a forum post and do all of this actually **voluntarily**
and not just *voluntarily*.

But to get back to the question: Of course I'm crazy. How else could I enjoy
this world?

See you soon, and good night!  
*Markus*
